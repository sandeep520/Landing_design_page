WHAT FEATURES YOU SHOULD CONSIDER WHEN CHOOSING AN AWNING?

TYPE OR STYLE

Permanent: attached to the building, solid frame, year-round, no storage issues, require less maintenace, less flexible, may require a building permit- for example, a gazebo, trellies or pergola cover.

Stationary: stays in one one location, usually fixed to a building, usually ready for use, often seasonal and less permanent, the fabric can be removed but time-consuming- for example a fixed frame window or door awning.

Retractable: more flexible, only out when needed, less permanent, attached to a building or free- standing, movable parts, no maintenace- a patio sun laterral arm retractable awning for example.

Freestanding: attached to a building but a deck, flexible location, can be permanent or temporary, generally portable, solid freamework, can have fixed or retratable fabric- a retractable pergola is an example.

MATERIAL

Fabric: awning fabric comes in cotton canvas, acrylic and solution dyed acrylic, polyester, vinyl or blended materials and is durable, easy to clean. Some qualities, such as mildew/fire/water resistant, waterproof and lightweight and lightweight may vary by fabric. Some fibers have better resistance to dust and dirt.

Metal: aluminum or steel lovers or plates, more permanent and less flexible than fabric, waterproof, mildew and fire resistant, durable, easy to clean.

Wood: less flexible and more permanent, louvers or roof, more maintance, durable.

Color: complimentry to your home or business, deflect pr attract heat, small patterns for small structures, large patterns for large structures, aesthetically pleasing.

UV Protection: reducing UV penetration reduces heat ubderneath the copy or inside the building creating a more comportable environment, and prevents fading of furniture, flooring, draperies etc.

Solid: reduces light penetration, create a viewing barrier, easier to waterproof, provides more protection from airborne debris.

Permeable: increased light penetration, increased viewing, less susceptible to windy condition, not waterproof, quicker drying, less obstractive.

MECHANICS

Manual: requires physical efort to turn the louvers,extend(open) or retract(close)the canopy, move or roll-up the awning.

Motorized: use electric motors instead of human power to operate the canopy at the flick of a switch or with a remote control, smartphone or tablet.

Automated- sun, wind, motion and rain sensor mechanisms that automatically open or close the awning, so it operates and your outdoor living area or commercial space is protected even when you're not there.

OTHER CONSIDERATION

Awning size: depends on the area requiring shade or rain protection, the direction it faces, or the number of doors and windows requiring shade- a 10 to 13-foot projection for east or west-facing building is usually enough for a patio set.

Shape: rectanuler,square, semi-circuler, trianguler,arched or a comnination are the common shapes.

Angle: affects the amount of light and sun penetration at different times of the day and season, reduces window protected with awning, and naturally sheds light or heavy precipitation(varies by product type.)
